Sound Off vs Captions

Captions is a slick, well-funded AI editor. Its own privacy policy also says it may generate biometric data from your face and voice, and train its AI on your videos. Sound Off does captions on your iPhone, and collects nothing.

"User Content" means your videos and photos. Captions' label says it's collected and linked to your identity. Sound Off's label says nothing is collected at all.

Your face, your voice, your videos

Captions is a US company, so this isn't about where your data goes. It's about what its own policy says it can do with your face, your voice, and your footage when you use its AI features.

"To enable certain features… we may generate biometric information from your face or voice."

Captions Privacy Policy

The policy says it uses your content to "develop, operate, improve… including to train our artificial intelligence/machine learning models," and may share it "to third parties for their own business and commercial purposes, including for… training their algorithms."

Captions Privacy Policy

To be precise: this is tied to using the AI features, and the policy frames the sharing as something you direct by choosing to use them. But the capability is right there in writing, your biometrics and your videos, used to train models. Sound Off has no such clause, because it never receives any of it.

On your phone, not their models

Sound Off transcribes, styles, and exports entirely on your iPhone, using Apple's on‑device speech model. Nothing is uploaded, nothing is linked to you, and nothing trains anyone's AI.

Pay once, not every month

Captions runs as a recurring subscription. Sound Off is a single purchase: you pay once and it's yours.

To be fair to Captions: it's a genuinely capable, polished AI tool with features Sound Off doesn't have, AI dubbing, eye contact correction, avatars. If that's what you want, it's a strong app. Sound Off isn't trying to match it feature for feature. It does one thing (stylish, accurate captions for vertical video) and it does that one thing without generating biometrics, training a model, or ever taking your video off your phone.
